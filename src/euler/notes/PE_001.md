# PE_001

The sum of multiples of 3 or 5 under a given limit is $sumOfMultiples(3) + sumOfMultiples(5) - sumOfMultiples(15)$, per the [Inclusion–exclusion principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inclusion%E2%80%93exclusion_principle).

This can be generalized for any arbitrary set of numbers - for numbers $n_1, n_2, ..., n_k$, the sum of multiples is:
$sumOfMultiples(n_1) + sumOfMultiples(n_2) + ... + sumOfMultiples(n_k) - $  
$sumOfMultiples(lcm(n_1, n_2)) - ... - sumOfMultiples(lcm(n_{k-1}, n_k)) + $  
$...$  
$(-1)^{k+1}sumOfMultiples(lcm(n_1, n_2, ..., n_k))$

Here, $lcm$ means the least common multiple of two numbers.

The count of multiples of $n$ under a limit $L$ is $\lfloor\frac{L}{n}\rfloor$. The sum of the first $x$ multiples of $n$ is:
$n + 2n + ... + xn = n(1 + 2 + ... + x) = n\frac{x(x+1)}{2}$.  
Therefore the sum of multiples of $n$ under a limit $L$ is $n\frac{c(c+1)}{2}$, where $c = \lfloor\frac{L}{n}\rfloor$.
