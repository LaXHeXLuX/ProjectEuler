# [PE_001](../PE_001.java)

The sum of multiples of 3 or 5 under a given limit is $\text{SOM}(3) + \text{SOM}(5) - \text{SOM}(15)$, per the [Inclusion–exclusion principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inclusion%E2%80%93exclusion_principle).

This can be generalized for any arbitrary set of numbers - for numbers $n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_k$, the sum of multiples is  
$$\text{SOM}(n_1) + \text{SOM}(n_2) + \ldots + \text{SOM}(n_k) - $$
$$\text{SOM}(\text{lcm}(n_1, n_2)) - \text{SOM}(\text{lcm}(n_1, n_3)) - \ldots - \text{SOM}(\text{lcm}(n_{k-1}, n_k)) + $$
$$\ldots$$
$$(-1)^{k+1}\text{SOM}(\text{lcm}(n_1, n_2, \ldots n_k))$$

Here, $\text{lcm}$ means the least common multiple of two numbers.

The count of multiples of $n$ under a limit $L$ is $\lfloor\frac{L}{n}\rfloor$. The sum of the first $x$ multiples of $n$ is
$$n + 2n + \ldots + xn = n(1 + 2 + \ldots + x) = n\frac{x(x+1)}{2}$$ 
Therefore the sum of multiples of $n$ under a limit $L$ is $n\frac{c(c+1)}{2}$, where $c = \lfloor\frac{L}{n}\rfloor$.
