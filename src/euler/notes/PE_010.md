# [PE_010](../PE_010.java)

The solution uses the [Meissel–Lehmer algorithm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meissel%E2%80%93Lehmer_algorithm#Expanding_%CF%86(x,_a)), inspired by [Lucy_Hedgehog's comment](https://projecteuler.net/thread=10;page=5#111677).  
To compute the prime sum up to a given limit $L$, first, primes up to $\sqrt{L}$ are generated using a sieve, and their sum is computed. Then we need the sum of all primes $p$, where $\sqrt{L} < p < L$. This is equivalent to asking for the sum of integers $i < L$, where $i$ has no prime factors smaller than $\sqrt{L}$, or alternatively, where $i$ has no prime factors smaller than $p_a$, with $p_a$ being the $a$-th prime and $a$ the amount of primes under $\sqrt{L}$.  
This sum $S(L, a)$ can be deconstructed as 
$$S(L, a-1) - p_{a-1} \cdot S(\frac{L}{p_{a-1}}, a-1)$$
i.e. "sum of all integers $i < L$ with no prime factors smaller than $p_{a-1}$, minus the sum of integers where $p_{a-1}$ is a prime factor".  
With base cases $S(L, 0) = \frac{L(L+1)}{2}$ (all integers $i < L$ are valid) and $S(0, a) = 0$ (no integers $i < L$ exist) we have a nice recursive structure where we can also implement memoization.
