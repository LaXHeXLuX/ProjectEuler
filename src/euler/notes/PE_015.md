# PE_015

Each unique path on an $n \times m$ grid visits $n+m$ points before the end. Each path will move down $n$ times and move right $m$ times.   
The problem may be rephrased as: out of $n+m$ moves, how many ways are there to pick $n$ moves for moving down (such that the order of the picks doesn't matter)?  
This is equivalent to the [$n\ \text{Choose}\ k$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binomial_coefficient) formula.
