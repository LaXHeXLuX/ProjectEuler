# [PE_028](../PE_028.java)

In an $n \times n$ number spiral, $n \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$, the biggest number will be $n^2$. The side length is $n$, giving the four outside corners $n^2$, $n^2 - (n-1)$, $n^2 - 2(n-1)$, $n^2 - 3(n-1)$. The sum of the four corners is $S(n) = 4n^2 - 6n + 6$.  
Then, the sum of all numbers on the diagonals in an $n \times n$ spiral is  
$$1 + S(3) + S(5) + \ldots + S(n) \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}S(2i+1) \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}4(2i+1)^2 - 6(2i+1) + 6 \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + \sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}16i^2 + 4i + 4 \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + 16\sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}i^2 + 4\sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}i + 4\sum_{i=1}^{\frac{n-1}{2}}1 \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + 16 \cdot \frac{\frac{n-1}{2}(\frac{n-1}{2}+1)(2\frac{n-1}{2}+1)}{6} + 4 \cdot \frac{\frac{n-1}{2}(\frac{n-1}{2}+1)}{2} + 4 \cdot \frac{n-1}{2} \equiv$$
$$\equiv 1 + 2 \cdot \frac{(n-1)n(n+1)}{3} + \frac{(n-1)(n+1)}{2} + 2 \cdot (n-1)$$
