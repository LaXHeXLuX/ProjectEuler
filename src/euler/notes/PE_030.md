# [PE_030](../PE_030.java)

Given a power $p$, a number $n$ with $d$ digits can at most have a digit power sum of $9^p \cdot d$. We want $n = \text{digitPowerSum}(n, p) \Rightarrow n \le \text{digitPowerSum}(n, p)$, where $n \ge 10^{d-1}$ and $\text{digitPowerSum}(n, p) \le 9^p \cdot d$, therefore $10^{d-1} \le n \le \text{digitPowerSum}(n, p) \le 9^p \cdot d \Rightarrow 10^{d-1} \le 9^p \cdot d$, providing an upper bound $d_{\text{max}}$ on $d$, which gives $n \le 9^p \cdot d_{\text{max}}$.

The digit powers are precomputed and all $n$, $10 \le n \le 9^p \cdot d_{\text{max}}$ are checked.
