# [PE_0313](../PE_0313.java)

Let $m \ge n$, the grid be of length $m$ and height $n$, and the initial position of the red counter be $(0, 0)$.

For $m > n$, $S(m, n) = (n-1) + (m-1) + 6(n-1) + 5(m-n-1) = 2n + 6m - 13$, because:
- It takes $(n-1) + (m-1)$ moves to move the empty space from the bottom right to where the red counter is, moving the red counter one tile right tp $(1, 0)$.
- When the empty space is next to the red counter, it takes $2$ moves to move the space to another side of the red counter, and $1$ move to move the counter itself to the empty space. Alternating between moving down and right, there are  $2 \cdot 3(n-1)$ moves to get the red counter to $(n-1, n)$ (since $m > n$, this is guaranteed to be within the grid).
- Now that the red counter is on the bottom row, it takes $4$ moves to move the empty space from the left side of the counter to the right side, and $1$ move to move the counter itself. Then it takes $5(m-n-1)$ additional moves to get the red counter to the bottom-right corner.

For $m = n$, the approach is similar, but we don't need the last stage of moving on the last row, and instead of taking $2 \cdot 3(n-1)$ moves to get there, it takes only $3(2n - 3)$. Then $S(m, n) = (n-1) + (m-1) + 3(2n-3) = 7n+m - 11 = 8m - 11$.

I do not know how to rigorously prove that this is  the most efficient method.

For a given prime $p$, this gives us two equations:
1) $2n + 6m - 13 = p^2$
2) $8m - 11 = p^2$

For $2n + 6m - 13 = p^2$,
- $n > 1 \Rightarrow m \le \lfloor \frac{p^2 + 10}{6} \rfloor$
- $n < m \Rightarrow m > \lfloor \frac{p^2 + 13}{8} \rfloor$

For $8m - 11 = p^2$, $m = \frac{p^2 + 11}{8}$.  
$p = 2$ will have no solutions for $8m - 11 = p^2$. Therefore, $p \equiv x \pmod{8}, x \in \{1, 3, 5, 7\}$. Then $p^2 \equiv x^2 \pmod{8}, x^2 \in \{1, 9, 25, 49\} \Rightarrow p^2 \equiv 1 \pmod{8}$. Then $p^2 + 11 \equiv 4 \pmod{8}$ can never be divisible by $8$, meaning we don't have to worry about $m = n$.

Then, for a given $p$, the number of $(m, n)$ so that $S(m, n) = p^2$ is equal to $2 \cdot (\lfloor \frac{p^2 + 12}{6} \rfloor - \lfloor \frac{p^2 + 13}{8} \rfloor)$.
