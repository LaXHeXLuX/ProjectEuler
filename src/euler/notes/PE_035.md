# [PE_035](../PE_035.java)

Given a limit $L$, all potential cyclic numbers are generated.  
Since no prime with more than one digit can end in $[0, 2, 4, 5, 6, 8]$, all digits of a cyclic prime must be in $[1, 3, 7, 9]$.  
Also, we can restrict any digit $d_i \ge d_0$, which is necessarily true for at least one element in a cyclic group.

Some results in the form  
`<L> <time in ms>: <cyclic number count>`

The speed quickly drops at $10^{10}$ due to the sudden need to handle `long` multiplication overflow.

It is [conjectured](https://oeis.org/A293663#:~:text=Conjecture%3A%20The%20sequence%20is%20finite.) (but as of 2026 not proved) that there are no cyclical primes above $1\ 000\ 000$, other than repunits.
