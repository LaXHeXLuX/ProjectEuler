# [PE_0357](../PE_0357.java)
 
If $n$ is a square, then $\sqrt{n}$ is a divisor of $n$. But $\sqrt{n} + n/\sqrt{n} = 2\sqrt{n}$ cannot be prime. Therefore, for $n$ to be a prime generating integer, it cannot be a square (except for $n = 1$).

Given that $n$ is not square, it will have an even number of divisors, and it suffices to check every divisor $d < \sqrt{n}$, since all divisors $d' > \sqrt{n}$ will "pair up" with the smaller divisors.

Every integer $n$ will have divisors $1$ and $n/1 = n$. Since $n+1$ must be prime, $n$ must be even (again, except for $n = 1$). 

Finally, if $n$ has a square part, i.e. $n = x \cdot i^2$ for $i > 1$, then $i$ is a divisor, and $i + n/i = i + xi = i(1 + x)$ is not prime.

Then $n$ must satisfy $n \equiv 2 \pmod{4}$ and be squarefree. As a fun fact, $\frac{6}{\pi^2}$ or roughly $60.8\%$ of numbers are squarefree. This means that even if you found a clever way to only iterate over squarefree numbers, you could only get a ${\sim}40\%$ speed boost.

It seems very likely that due to always needing to check $n+1$ for primality, you cannot avoid sieving up to the limit, which gives a hard $O(N \log\log N + M)$ cap on the runtime, where $N \log \log N$ is the time complexity for generating the sieve, and $M$ is everything else.
