# [PE_040](../PE_040.java)

The $i$-th digit in the sequence can be found in logarithmic time. Note that for any $d$, there are precisely $9 \cdot 10^{d-1}$ $d$-digit numbers. 
Then you can iterate $d$, each time subtracting $d \cdot 9 \cdot 10^{d-1}$ from $i$, until $i < d \cdot 9 \cdot 10^{d-1}$. Then the number which contains the $i$-th digit is $10^{d-1} + \frac{i}{d}$, and the $i$-th digit is the $n$-th digit of that number, where $i = n \pmod{d}$.
