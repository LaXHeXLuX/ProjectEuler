# [PE_069](../PE_069.java)

The totient function is calculated as
$$\phi(n) = n \prod_{p \vert n} (1 - \frac{1}{p})$$
meaning 
$$n/\phi(n) = \frac{1}{\prod_{p \vert n} (1 - \frac{1}{p})}$$
Then, to maximise $n/\phi(n)$, we want to minimize
$$\prod_{p \vert n} (1 - \frac{1}{p})$$
Given a limit $L$, for $n \le L$, this quotient is minimized by requiring prime factors to be distinct and as small as possible. Therefore, the answer is the largest primorial not greater than $L$.
